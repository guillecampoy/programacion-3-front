# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Students and reviewers using a browser-only university prototype for an online food store. They log in, browse the catalog, manage a cart, and exercise the protected client/admin flows without a backend.

## Product Purpose

Demonstrate a simple food-store experience with authentication, role-based routing, product browsing, cart management, and basic admin maintenance views. Success means the app is easy to understand, stable, and clearly shows the intended workflow for the assignment.

## Brand Personality

Simple, modern, smooth. The interface should feel calm, practical, and dependable, with no unnecessary flourish.

## Anti-references

Avoid anything extravagant, ornamental, or hard to use. This is a simple prototype for a food store, so the interface should not feel cluttered, flashy, or clever for its own sake. It should also not feel overdesigned in ways that hide the fact that the product is intentionally lightweight and frontend-only.

## Design Principles

- Make the primary task obvious on every screen.
- Prefer familiar patterns over custom interactions.
- Keep the same component vocabulary across login, catalog, cart, and admin views.
- Use feedback to clarify state, not decoration.
- Optimize for quick comprehension in a university demo context.
- Keep the frontend-only constraints visible through simplicity, not apology.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

No special accessibility requirements were requested. Meet baseline contrast, keyboard navigation, visible focus, clear labels, and readable form controls. If motion is used later, it should not block task completion and should respect reduced-motion preferences.
